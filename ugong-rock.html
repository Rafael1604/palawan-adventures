Ugong Rock in Palawan is a 75 foot(23-meter) limestone rock formation located in Barangay Tagabinet, Puerto Princesa. It is a popular eco-tourism site known for spelunking, rock climbing, and ziplining. The name "Ugong" comes from the echoing sound ("ugong" in Filipino) produced when tapping the limestone walls inside the cave. Visitors can explore the cave's narrow passages, rock formations, and a viewing deck at the top, which offers a scenic view of the surrounding rice fields and forests. It is often visited as part of a Puerto Princesa Underground River tour, as it is located along the route to the famous UNESCO World Heritage Site. .
