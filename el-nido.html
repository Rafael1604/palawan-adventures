El Nido is known for its beautiful beaches and stunning landscapes. El Nido is a coastal town in the Philippines on the northern tip of Palawan Island. It's a popular destination for tourists known for its white-sand beaches, turquoise waters, and limestone cliffs. El Nido is part of the Bacuit Archipelago, which includes 45 islands and islets. El Nido's limestone cliffs are similar to those in Ha Long Bay, Vietnam.

El Nido is located in the northern part of Palawan, Philippines. El Nido encompasses over 40 islands peppered across the Bacuit Archipelago, each with its own unique geological wonders but the same natural majesty. No wonder El Nido has earned nothing but praise from visitors and numerous accolades for being one of the best island and beach destinations in the world. El Nido has also seen a rise of numerous establishments over the years, accommodating record-high tourist arrivals in the area of about 150,000 in a year. Exploring the tourist spots in El Nido is mostly done outdoors, so weather conditions are the primary considerations in planning when to visit the town and its surrounding island
